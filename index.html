Emergency Services

These services are available 24/7 to provide immediate medical care to individuals with life-threatening conditions, severe injuries, or sudden illnesses. Emergency departments (EDs) are equipped to stabilize patients, conduct initial assessments, and provide critical interventions.

Inpatient Care

Inpatient services involve admitting patients to the hospital for more extended periods of care, typically involving surgeries, medical treatments, or recovery from serious illnesses. Patients receive 24-hour medical attention, nursing care, and access to various medical specialties.

Surgical Services

Hospitals offer a range of surgical procedures, from routine surgeries like appendectomies to complex procedures like organ transplants. Surgical services include pre-operative evaluations, the actual surgical procedure, post-operative care, and recovery support.

Outpatient Care

Outpatient services are provided to patients who don't require overnight hospitalization. This includes routine medical check-ups, consultations with specialists, diagnostic tests, minor surgical procedures, and therapies such as physiotherapy or chemotherapy.

Laboratory Services

Hospital labs perform a wide array of tests on blood, urine, tissue samples, and other bodily fluids. These tests aid in diagnosing diseases, monitoring health conditions, and assessing the effectiveness of treatments.

Maternity and Obstetric Care

Hospitals offer services for pregnant women, including prenatal care, labor and delivery rooms, postpartum care, and neonatal intensive care units (NICUs) for premature or sick newborns.
